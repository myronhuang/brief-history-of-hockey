A Brief History of Hockey

A Tribute to Canada's National Pastime

600 BC

Archaeological evidence from Greece depicts an ancient ball and stick game being played. The game is named “kerētízein  (κερητίζειν)”, after the horn-like stick used (kéras, κέρας).

1500s

IJscolf, a game resembling golf on ice, was popular in the coastal region of northwestern Europe.

1600s

"A vehement frost continued from Martinmas [St. Martin's Day, November 11] till the 20th of February. The sea froze so far as it ebbed, and sundry went into ships upon ice and played at the chamiare [another word for shinty, a Scottish game resembling hockey] a mile within the sea-mark."


- David Calderwood (Excerpt from "The Historie of the Kirk of Scotland", 1646)

1700s

“I must now describe to you the game of Hockey; we have each a stick turning up at the end. We get a bung. There are two sides one of them knocks one way and the other side the other way. If any one of the sides makes the bung reach that end of the churchyard it is victorious.”


- William Pierre Le Cocq (Excerpt from a 1799 letter)

1800s

On March 3, 1875, Canadian James Creighton organized the first indoor game at Montreal's Victoria Skating Rink. The match was played  between two nine-player teams, including James Creighton and several McGill University students.

1900s

The National Hockey League was founded on November 26th, 1917. The first NHL game was played at Montreal Arena on December 19th, 1917 at 8:15 P.M. EST between the Toronto Hockey Club and the Montreal Wanderers.

May 17th 1991

In game 2 of the 1991 Stanley Cup Finals between the Pittsburgh Penguins and the Minnesota North Stars, Penguins Captain Mario Lemieux scored one of the greatest goals in NHL history. The Penguins would win the game by a score of 4 - 1 and would go on to win the series in 6 games.
